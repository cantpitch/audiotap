tapencoder.dll implements the TAP encoder for audio data. The TAP encoding format is a lossy compressed audio format which efficiently encodes the audio on Commodore 64 tapes. It was invented by Per Håkan Sundell and is described here. The encoding algorithm is by Fabrizio Gennari, derived by one made by Janne Veli Kujala, who in his turn derived it from the one used by Andreas Matthies in Tape64.

tapdecoder.dll implements the TAP decoder for audio data. The algorithm for decoding TAP data to square, sinusoidal or triangular waves is by Fabrizio Gennari. The use of square waves was inspired by Tomaz Kac's 64TAPVOC, and confirmed after discussions with Luigi Di Fraia.
