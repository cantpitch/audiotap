First of all, it is better to open the Volume Control (double click on the speaker in the system tray), then select Options/Properties and choose "Adjust volume for Recording". Then, select Line in and unselect everything else. Set the volume of Line in to the maximum.

If you are recording directly, tune the volume of the tape player so the Volume level bar is about 1/2 - 3/4 of the maximum. If it is too close to the maximum the sound will be distorted.

If you are recording with a wave editor, inspect the waveform after recording. If it is a nice and smooth sinusoid, everything should be fine.

If it is too close to the extremes, try lowering the volume of the tape player.

If there are irregularities in the waveform, try to set Minimum height in the advanced options to a value higher than the irregularities' height, but lower than the waveform's height. Remember that 255 is the maximum height of the waveform.
